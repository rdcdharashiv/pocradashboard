🔍 Key Observations & Inferences
📈
Farmer Enrollment: Strong +20.4% Growth
District registered 1,847 new farmers in 9 days (13→22 Apr). Dharashiv leads with +106 new farmers. All 8 talukas showed consistent 3–5% farmer growth, indicating active field-level enrollment drives.
🚀
D1 Farmer Verification: Explosive +506% Surge
Farmer Verification (D1) surged from 99 to 600 (+501). This signals a massive operational push at the entry-level verification stage. If this pace continues, pipeline entry will accelerate significantly.
✅
Final Payment (D8+D9): Significant Improvement
Accounts Officer (D8): +135 (+49.5%). Sub-Div AO Final (D9): +70 (+57.9%). These are strong positive signals indicating actual DBT payments reaching farmers are accelerating.
⚠️
Tuljapur: Highest Application Growth (+10.2%)
Tuljapur recorded the highest application growth rate at +10.2% (1,937→2,134). Combined with +5.5% farmer growth, it shows strong demand generation momentum that merits scaling support resources.
📊
Activity Coverage: Villages Count Added
New dataset now includes village-level activity coverage. Sprinkler covers 127 villages, Drip 124, Recharge of Well 109. These numbers confirm irrigation activities have the widest geographic spread.

⚠️ Concern & Risk Areas
🔴
RISK 2: Payment Rate Still Critical (3.3%)
Despite D8/D9 improvements, payment rate remains critically low. 14,374 applications but only ~476 paid. D9 at 191 is still less than 1.5% of total applications. DBT disbursement remains a severe bottleneck.
⚠️
RISK 3: D5 Spot Check Decline (−28%)
Asst. AO Spot Check (D5) fell from 157 to 113 (−44). This mid-pipeline quality verification stage is weakening, which could compromise the integrity of applications moving to final payment stages.
⚠️
RISK 4: D3 Site Inspections Declining (−5.4%)
Asst. AO Site Inspections (D3) fell from 3,067 to 2,902 (−165). While minor, this indicates field officer capacity constraints. With rising application load (+727 new), D3 throughput must be increased.
